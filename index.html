Human Rights

You benefit from systems
that violate them.

You're thinking:

"Not me."

Yes, you.

You didn't build it.

You didn't design it.

You live inside it.

Clothes.

Phones.

Food.

Convenience has a cost.

Human rights are not just violated by governments.

They are sustained by systems.

1948

After World War II, the world made a promise.

"Never again."

"Everyone has rights."

That’s the claim.

Not the reality.

Rights are written down.

Power decides when they matter.

Those are not the same thing.

You decide.

A government removes ONE right to stay in control.

Freedom of speech
Privacy
Fair trial
Education

It is now gone.

At first… nothing changes for you.

Then small changes. Not to you.

You accept it.

You notice. You disagree. You continue anyway.

The system doesn't need your support. Only your acceptance.

Eventually… it reaches you.

You speak. You resist. But that right is gone.

You wait for others. They are waiting too.

Millions protest.

Days pass. Weeks pass. The system adjusts.

The headlines change. The outrage fades.

You see it less. But it didn't stop.

For a moment…

it feels like change.

You watch.
You scroll.

You continue.
