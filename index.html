Quick question.

If your government takes away your freedom…

is that illegal?

1948

After World War II, the world had a problem:

How do you stop governments from abusing their own people?

The Universal Declaration of Human Rights

A global agreement that says:

“Every human being deserves dignity, freedom, and protection.”

Key Idea

Human rights are:

Universal
Everyone
Inalienable
Cannot be taken away
Equal
No exceptions

Some Rights

Freedom of speech
Right to education
Protection from torture
Right to a fair trial

Now it gets uncomfortable.

These rights exist on paper.

Do they exist in reality?

Mini Debate (5 mins)

In pairs, choose ONE:

Is free speech unlimited?

Reality Check

Human rights are not enforced by magic.

They only exist if people defend them.

Final Question
